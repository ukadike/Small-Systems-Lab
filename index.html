What this is

Small Systems Lab (SSL) is a public-interest studio for tender systems: civic tools, editorial intelligence, accessibility infrastructure, food memory, environmental sensing, speculative storytelling, and AI literacy. SSL treats care, documentation, rule-based intelligence, and accessibility as infrastructure — not as decoration or afterthought.

Method

SSL builds systems around five linked practices.

1. Operations of Care

The workflows, roles, rituals, safeguards, and review loops that keep a system accountable: intake, consent, documentation, review, escalation, publishing, archiving, correction, access support, community feedback, maintenance.

2. Rule-Based Intelligence

Explicit rules for consent, access, risk, provenance, translation, publishing, repair, and override. SSL treats intelligence as governance, not magic — the machine may assist, classify, translate, or summarize, but it does not replace human judgment.

3. Ancient Geometry as Interface Logic

Circles, grids, triangles, spirals, ratios, and thresholds used as organizing principles, not decoration: consent loops, taxonomies, memory, revision, calm proportion, and the moment between observe and publish.

4. Accessibility as Architecture

Blind editor access, keyboard-only use, captions, transcripts, high contrast, plain language, and tactile and multimodal outputs — built in from the start, not added at the end.

5. Public Knowledge Stewardship

Preserving memory, context, local testimony, and civic information for communities and future histories. A system succeeds only if information can return to the people it concerns.

System Questions

Every SSL project asks:

  • Who is this system for?
  • Who can be harmed by it?
  • What rules govern it?
  • What consent is required?
  • What evidence is missing?
  • What should not be published?
  • What must remain accessible?
  • What gets archived for future review?
  • What geometry or pattern helps people understand the system?
  • What happens when the system fails?
