# Small Systems Lab

**Small Systems Lab (SSL)** is a public-interest studio for tender systems: civic tools, editorial intelligence, accessibility infrastructure, food memory, environmental sensing, speculative storytelling, and AI literacy.

Founded by **Adekemi (Kemi) Sijuwade Ukadike**, SSL treats care, documentation, rule-based intelligence, and accessibility as infrastructure.

## Core Method

SSL builds systems around five linked practices:

1. **Operations of Care** — the workflows, roles, rituals, safeguards, and review loops that keep a system accountable.
2. **Rule-Based Intelligence** — explicit rules for consent, access, risk, provenance, translation, publishing, repair, and override.
3. **Ancient Geometry as Interface Logic** — circles, grids, triangles, spirals, ratios, thresholds, and patterns used as organizing principles, not decoration.
4. **Accessibility as Architecture** — blind editor access, keyboard-only use, captions, transcripts, high contrast, plain language, tactile and multimodal outputs.
5. **Public Knowledge Stewardship** — preserving memory, context, local testimony, and civic information for communities and future histories.

## System Questions

Every SSL project asks:

- Who is this system for?
- Who can be harmed by it?
- What rules govern it?
- What consent is required?
- What evidence is missing?
- What should not be published?
- What must remain accessible?
- What gets archived for future review?
- What geometry or pattern helps people understand the system?
- What happens when the system fails?
